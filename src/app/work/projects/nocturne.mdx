## Overview

NOCTURNE — *Gotham After Dark* is an immersive, single-page Batman experience, built to compete for an Awwwards weekly pick. The brief I set myself: a landing page good enough to sit among Awwwards' picks of the week — dark, cinematic, heavy on motion, and a genuinely deployable product rather than a static mockup. No generic templates, no AI-template look.

---

## What I built

A self-contained scroll experience. It opens on a "signal acquired" preloader, then drops you into a hero where the Bat-Signal is projected into the fog — a glowing halo, the emblem, and a soft beam washing down over the headline. Rain falls on a canvas layer, film grain sits over the whole page, and a magnetic custom cursor reacts to whatever you hover.

From there it moves through five acts:

- **Manifesto** — a quote that reveals word by word as you scroll.
- **Arsenal** — a pinned section that scrolls sideways through blueprint-style schematics of the suit, the Batarang, the grapple gun, the Batmobile, and the cowl.
- **Rogues Gallery** — each villain's threat file follows the cursor on hover.
- **City** — counters that tick up over a parallax skyline.
- **Finale** — you light the signal yourself, with a status readout that comes online.

Every illustration is hand-built SVG — no stock images, no icon packs. The bat emblem, the skyline and the gear schematics are all drawn in code, which keeps the whole site down to a handful of files.

---

## How it's built

Plain HTML, CSS and vanilla JavaScript — no framework, no build step. Motion runs on GSAP: ScrollTrigger for the scroll-driven sequences, ScrollSmoother for smooth scrolling and parallax, SplitText for the type reveals. The horizontal Arsenal uses GSAP's `containerAnimation`, so the sideways gallery stays tied to vertical scroll position. Typography pairs Big Shoulders Display with Archivo and JetBrains Mono — chosen to read like a Gotham case file instead of a default Google-Fonts combo.

---

## Production-ready — the part most landing pages skip

After the visual work, I treated it like a product going to production:

- **Accessibility** — keyboard focus styles, a skip link, screen-reader text for the hover-only content, real button state (`aria-pressed`, a live status region), and a full `prefers-reduced-motion` path that cuts the heavy motion for people who ask for it.
- **Mobile** — a proper slide-in menu, tap fallbacks for the hover interactions, and parallax that still works on touch.
- **Performance** — the canvas rain is batched and frame-capped, the expensive blur was trimmed, and animation loops pause when off-screen.
- **Deployment** — subresource-integrity hashes on the CDN scripts, Open Graph and Twitter cards with a generated share image, a favicon, and a graceful fallback if a script fails to load.

## Review & outcome

To catch what I'd miss on my own, I ran the finished build through a review across seven angles: design, motion, accessibility, performance, mobile, code correctness and deployability. It surfaced 52 issues; 37 held up after verification, and I fixed all 37 before calling it done.
